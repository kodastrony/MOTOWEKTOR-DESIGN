- **Export markets:** Exports to **Germany, the Czech Republic, Slovakia, and the Baltic states**; products serve the Central European market.

# Part B — Industry Context & Competitors

## 1. Market Overview — Polish & European Van/Truck Body Conversion

**Poland as a bodybuilding hub.** Poland is a major Central/Eastern European automotive manufacturing base and an important European transport hub, historically serving as a "low-cost base" and key link in the European automotive value chain, supplying vehicles and components primarily to Germany, the Czech Republic, France and the UK (Trade.gov.pl). This cost advantage plus proximity to Western Europe has made Poland fertile ground for light commercial vehicle (LCV) bodybuilders — many of whom export. MotoWektor itself exports to Germany, Czechia, Slovakia and the Baltics, consistent with this pattern.

**How the segment works.** Body conversion is a "Stage 2 manufacturer" business: the OEM (Renault, Fiat/Stellantis, Mercedes, VW, Ford, Iveco, etc.) ships a chassis/cab or panel van, and the bodybuilder mounts the body (box, container, refrigerated/isothermal unit, tipper, flatbed, platform, workshop fit-out, etc.). In Poland a converted vehicle requires re-homologation ("homologacja po zabudowie") — technical approval that the modified vehicle is road-safe. Builders who hold standing manufacturer approvals can issue homologation certificates directly, which is a meaningful competitive moat.

**Demand drivers.** The most common base vehicles across Europe are the Fiat Ducato, Iveco Daily, Mercedes Sprinter, VW Crafter, Opel Movano, Renault Master and Peugeot Boxer. Growth is driven by booming e-commerce/last-mile delivery demand for vans, fleet renewal after the chip-crisis supply recovery, and the shift to electrified models.

**Market sizing — treat with caution.** Third-party "van conversion market" reports cite figures like a global market of ~USD 8.1bn (2024, Cognitive Market Research) and a European van *market* of USD 81.2bn (2021) → USD 142.6bn (2031) at ~5.6% CAGR (Allied Market Research); EU electric van sales jumped 56.8% in 2023 vs 2022. **Confidence: low-to-moderate** — these aggregator reports blend camper/RV conversions with commercial bodybuilding and vary wildly in scope, so they are directional context only, not a precise read on MotoWektor's addressable market.

## 2. Notable Competitors

**Polish bodybuilders (closest peers):**
- **Widar** (Żórawina, nr Wrocław) — aluminum-focused builder (boxes, containers, isothermal, tippers); claims 10,000+ conversions and 2,200+ clients; also does collision repair. PL.
- **Gruau Polska** (PL arm of France's Groupe Gruau) — "European leader," Stage 2 manufacturer with approvals from Renault, Citroën, Opel, Peugeot, Ford, Dacia, Fiat, MAN, Mercedes, VW; refrigerated/iso, buses, crew/armored, accessibility vehicles, fleet contracts for uniformed services. PL/FR.
- **Bemet** — 20+ years; steel & aluminum autolawety (car transporters) and mobile workshops. PL.
- **BLYSS** — container, box and flatbed conversions for commercial vehicles. PL.
- **Pronar BOTT / zabudowy-samochodowe.pl** — workshop/van-racking conversions using the BOTT Vario3 system; multi-branch (Wrocław, Poznań, Warsaw, Kraków, Szczecin, Lublin, Zabrze). PL (BOTT is DE-origin racking).
- **DD Zabudowy, AutoHauser, Nadwozia-Partner, Knautech, Pony Auto, Modesto Polska, Baster (Kraków)** — small/mid PL builders covering boxes, buses, service vans and homologation. PL.

- **Sortimo** (Germany) — market-leading van racking + strong e-mobility consulting and an online configurator; OEM conversion partnerships (e.g., LEVC VN5 electric van). DE.

**Larger Western European bodybuilders (benchmark tier):**

## 3. Where MotoWektor Sits & How It Differentiates

- **Heritage & scale** — spun off from Wektor Sp. z o.o. (active in automotive since 1992); MotoWektor itself active since 2008; **120 production employees** across three production halls. This puts it solidly in the **mid-to-upper tier of Polish builders** — larger and longer-established than most small PL shops, though below pan-European players like Gruau or Paneltex.
- **Breadth of range** — boxes (open/cargo), aluminum containers, isothermal & refrigerated units, tippers, flatbeds, platforms, plus specialized builds (food trucks, mobile workshops, refrigerated catering, car/machinery transporters, showrooms). This wide catalog is a genuine differentiator vs. niche aluminum-only or tipper-only competitors.
- **OEM approvals** — products are authorized by **Renault Polska, Fiat Auto Poland, Citroën, Peugeot and Iveco**, with "świadectwo homologacji" (homologation certificates) for bodies on their chassis. This matters competitively: it lets MotoWektor deliver factory-grade, dealer-channel "pod klucz" (turnkey) vehicles. *Confidence note:* Mercedes/VW/Ford are **not** listed among its approvals (Gruau, by contrast, lists those), a possible gap.
- **Own IP / engineering** — patented pneumatic lifting system (2010), proprietary **PSM mounting system**, roof spoilers, sleeping cabs — signaling in-house R&D rather than pure assembly.
- **Digital edge** — MotoWektor already advertises an **online configurator called "TechnikPRO."** Among Polish peers this is relatively rare; it aligns the brand with the digital direction Western leaders like Sortimo have set.

**Net positioning:** a vertically-integrated, OEM-approved Polish bodybuilder competing on **range breadth + manufacturer homologations + in-house engineering**, exporting into the German-speaking/CEE corridor. Its closest direct rivals are Widar (aluminum/scale) and Gruau Polska (international group, broader OEM approvals). *Confidence: moderate* — claims are drawn from company sites and are not independently audited here.

## 4. Industry Trends Relevant to a Modern Website

- **Online configurators are becoming table stakes.** MotoWektor's TechnikPRO configurator is a strong asset to foreground on a redesigned site (lead-gen + qualification).
- **E-mobility / electric van bodies.** EU electric van sales rose 56.8% (2023). EV upfitting is *not* a carryover from ICE — builders must respect factory-approved attachment points and "no-drill zones," manage payload against battery mass, and avoid high-voltage routing. A modern site can credibly signal "EV-ready bodies" on electric Master/Sprinter/Crafter platforms (subject to confirming MotoWektor's actual capability).
- **OEM/dealer channel trust signals.** Prominently displaying homologation certificates and OEM approval logos is a proven conversion-rate trust lever in this category.
- **Refrigerated / temperature-controlled demand** (cold-chain, food, pharma last-mile) and **sustainable/lightweight materials** (aluminum, modular interiors) are recurring growth themes worth merchandising.
- **Fleet & warranty messaging** — competitors lead with multi-year warranties and fleet delivery reliability; matching/beating this on the site reduces friction for B2B fleet buyers.
